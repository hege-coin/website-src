This was just insane. 2,222 hand-drawn Hegends, ~$100 each at launch, sold out
in like an hour. This was a huge success and helped kick-start our marketing
budget, but what caused the NFTs to sell this fast? Easy. REAL utility. Buybacks
and rewards. Here's how it works:

### NFT Bank

- When the Hegends were minted (purchased at launch), a portion of the minting fees was deposited into a special NFT bank
- This NFT bank is used to perform buybacks, driving up the price of $HEGE

### Monthly distributions

- Each month, 5% of the Hege tokens in the bank are distributed among NFT holders
- The distribution is proportional: if you hold more NFTs, you receive more tokens

### Rarity tiers & multipliers

- The NFTs are categorized into 5 rarity tiers: Common, Uncommon, Rare, Epic, Legendary
- Depending on tier, the multiplier of rewards is different:
  1. Common: 1.0√ó
  1. Uncommon: 1.5√ó
  1. Rare: 2.5√ó
  1. Epic: 5.0√ó
  1. Legendary: 10.0√ó

### Royalties / transactions

- When an NFT is bought or sold, an 8% royalty is applied
- Of that 8%, half goes to the project wallet, and half goes into the NFT bank (which helps fund future token purchases and thus future rewards)

### Eligibility / constraints

- NFTs that are listed for sale on the distribution date are not eligible for the rewards. So you can‚Äôt have your NFT on sale in the marketplace during that snapshot / distribution period
- The wallet that holds the NFT must also hold at least 100 HEGE tokens
